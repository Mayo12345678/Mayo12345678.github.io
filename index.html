Introduction

Everyday we encounter and interact with many forces. Understanding the forces that act on us and the objects around us will give us a better sense of how the world works and how things move. Forces are applied in a particular direction, and they have different sizes which determines how much force is acting on an object. The unit which we measure force are Newtons, and their symbol is 'N.' This unit is named after Sir Isaac Newton who was a scientist who came up with three laws of motion which describe forces. Force Problems

In the Mechanics exam, it is likely to you will get problems asking you to draw free body diagrams of certain objects and what forces are acting on them. The key things to remember are
  • that you use a ruler to draw arrows of the magnitude of force
  • always start the arrows from the center of the object
  • always draw the arrows size in proportion to the magnitude i.e. a 5000N arrow would be larger then a 3000N arrow
